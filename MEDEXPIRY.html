Medicine Knowledge: When to Take and Avoid

Find out when to take your medications and when to avoid them, along with possible side effects and drug interactions.

  • Paracetamol: Safe to take for mild pain and fever. Avoid if you have liver disease.
  • Ibuprofen: Use for inflammation and pain relief. Avoid if you have stomach ulcers or are pregnant.
  • Antibiotics: Take only when prescribed. Avoid if allergic or without a doctor's supervision.
  • Aceclofenac: To relieve pain and inflammation in conditions such as rhematoid arthritis, ankylosing spondylitis and osteoarthritis.
  • Diclofenac: Is a Nonsteroidal Anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID) used to treat mild-to-moderate pain, and helps to relieve symptoms of arthritis (eg, osteoarthritis or rheumatoid arthritis), such as inflammation, swelling, stiffness, and joint pain.
  • cetirizine: Is used to temporarily relieve the symptoms of hay fever and allergy to other substances. These symptoms include sneezing; runny nose; itchy, red, watery eyes; and itchy nose or throat.
